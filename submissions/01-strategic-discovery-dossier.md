## Problem summary

Legacy-Trust Bank have invested into modernising their systems, however the debt recovery process has not evolved accordingly. As a result, agents have developed manual workarounds, including spreadsheets, to be able to track and update delinquent accounts. However, adelinquent account volumes have increased to over 100,000, managers have lost visibility of account journeys, and customers have been subject to longer resolution times. Financially, the bank loses around 15% revenue through delayed action and failed collections. Operationally, straightforward cases tend to take 8 minutes longer than ideal to resolve on average, putting a strain of the collections team by diverting agent capacity away from higher-value tasks.


## Stakeholder overview

| Stakeholder group | What they care about | How success is measured | Main worry | Evidence they will trust |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Operations leadership | Improving the efficiency and capacity of the debt recovery team | Agent time recovered, self-serve resolution rate | A future-state process that is efficient on paper but is impractical to actually follow | Time spent per account; missed follow-up rates; duplicate contact attempts |
| Team leaders and agents | Having the As-Is process documented accurately for leadership | Reduction in duplicated work; hand-off efficiency; clear swimlanes between the system and agents | Smart-Recovery reflects an ideal documented process rather than the actual processes in place now | Bottlenecks in the As-Is process: duplicate contact attempts; cross-source information reconciliation |
| Finance | Reducing revenue leakage caused by operational inefficiencies | Transparent financial model with sensitivity-tested ROI projections | Blind trust in Smart-Recovery and failure to realise financial promises | Data directly tied to financial figures: follow-up completion rates; duplicate work; account age-out rate |
| Product and delivery | Having a solution that clearly links current pain points to future-state workflows | A discovery package that clearly traces pain points to workflows | Poorly evidenced, partially formed requirements, recommendations | A workflow mapping that exposes issues at scale, backed by data |
| Customers | Clear and simple ways to understand what they owe | Faster case resolution; visibility of information; proof of progress between contacts | The portal failing to clearly layout information on balance or payment options, requiring human clarification | Consistent information across channels, reduced waiting times and fewer repeat contacts (inferred from operational data)|

## Discovery questions

- Which aspects of the current process are most difficult to monitor and measure?
- Which process issues are responsible for missed follow-ups?
- Where do managers lack visibility in managing and tracking accounts?
- What are the most common scenarios in which a case which requires further agent intervention?
- Which process delays most directly contribute to accounts aging out?
- What proportion of the workload consists of straightforward cases, and cases requiring human judgment?
- How much more time and effort do the current workarounds take when compared to an ideal process?
