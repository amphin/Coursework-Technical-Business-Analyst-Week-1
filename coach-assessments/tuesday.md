### Situation
Hartwell Private Health is a wealth management firm managing over 4,000 clients. The firm recently migrated from a legacy CRM system to a new cloud-based platform 18 months ago.

### Complication
Following an FCA review, Hartwell was informed that a number of their client records had incomplete or outdated KYC documentation. The investigation revealed that while compliance documents had been migrated into the new CRM, critical metadata used for validation and review had not. This resulted in approximately 1,100 client records, with 300 to 500 records requiring immediate remediation. This issue creates regulatory risks under AML, MiFID II and CASS requirements.

### Question
How can Hartwell rapidly remediate affected records and satisfy FCA expections within the 90-day deadline, while implementing controls to prevent similar compliance failures in the future?

### Answer
Hartwell should establish a structured programme that prioritises remediation of high-risk client records, documenting the exact points of failure which resulted in the issue to occur, and introducing stronger data and process governance for future migrations.
