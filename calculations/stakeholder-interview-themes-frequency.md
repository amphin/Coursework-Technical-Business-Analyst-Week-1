# Stakeholder Interview Themes (By Frequency)

Source: `Legacy-Trust-Data/stakeholder_interview_notes.csv` (126 notes)

Method used: each note was assigned to one primary theme using consistent keyword-based coding, then ranked by frequency.

## Top 5 themes

| Rank | Theme | Count | Share |
|---|---|---:|---:|
| 1 | Workflow inefficiency, backlog, and poor prioritisation | 40 | 31.7% |
| 2 | Data fragmentation, visibility, and reporting gaps | 28 | 22.2% |
| 3 | Self-service opportunity with limits for complex cases | 22 | 17.5% |
| 4 | Legacy platform constraints and workaround-driven operations | 22 | 17.5% |
| 5 | Change governance, measurement, and value realisation concerns | 14 | 11.1% |

## Theme detail and examples

### 1) Workflow inefficiency, backlog, and poor prioritisation (40)
Recurring signals:
- Cases stall in callback states and queues.
- Work is not sequenced strategically (random contact order, mixed simple/complex queues).
- Handoffs and shift transitions cause follow-up loss and delays.

Representative notes:
- SN-003: "Simple cases that could be resolved in minutes take days because they get stuck in the wrong queue."
- SN-053: "Cases get stuck in 'pending callback' status indefinitely because the callback date is never enforced."
- SN-105: "We lose at least 20% of follow-ups because they fall between shifts and no one owns the handoff."

### 2) Data fragmentation, visibility, and reporting gaps (28)
Recurring signals:
- Audit trail and status data are inconsistent across tools.
- Managers and finance lack trusted, timely reporting.
- Teams cannot reliably diagnose why cases fail or stall.

Representative notes:
- SN-008: "The audit trail is scattered across email, spreadsheets, and the legacy database, making compliance reviews a nightmare."
- SN-012: "The data quality is so poor that we stopped running management reports altogether."
- SN-070: "The finance team cannot forecast recovery revenue because they do not trust the activity data."

### 3) Self-service opportunity with limits for complex cases (22)
Recurring signals:
- Strong potential for customer self-management where cases are straightforward.
- Customer control/transparency repeatedly linked to better response.
- Clear boundary conditions where human handling remains necessary.

Representative notes:
- SN-001: "Customers respond well when they have control and transparency about what they owe."
- SN-082: "Self-service works well for straightforward cases, but we need to be honest about which ones those are."
- SN-121: "We have edge cases involving hardship, vulnerability, and regulatory forbearance that need human judgment."

### 4) Legacy platform constraints and workaround-driven operations (22)
Recurring signals:
- Core systems are outdated relative to volume and product complexity.
- Agent productivity depends on informal workarounds.
- Manual effort (re-keying, spreadsheet reliance) consumes operational capacity.

Representative notes:
- SN-016: "The old system was designed for half the volume we handle now, and it shows."
- SN-061: "The 20-year-old system was built for a different business model entirely."
- SN-111: "Every status update in the old system requires manual re-keying into the spreadsheet."

### 5) Change governance, measurement, and value realisation concerns (14)
Recurring signals:
- Stakeholders need clear proof of impact before full rollout.
- Compliance and process assurance are gating criteria.
- Benefits framing is inconsistent across leadership, operations, and finance.

Representative notes:
- SN-019: "I cannot recommend automating anything until I see that it will not create new compliance risks."
- SN-043: "We need to measure whether this is better before we commit to rolling it out."
- SN-095: "Managers need a clear view of whether self-service actually reduces workload."

## Quick takeaway

The dominant concern is operational flow breakdown (31.7%), followed by fragmented data/reporting (22.2%). Together, these two themes account for 53.9% of all interview signals, indicating that process and information integrity issues are currently a bigger blocker than feature capability alone.
